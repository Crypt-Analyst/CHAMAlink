Meet Bilford Bwire

The 25-year-old visionary behind ChamaLink's mission to revolutionize African community finance

The Beginning

It all started in 2007, when I was just 8 years old during the challenging siku za Moi era. I witnessed my grandmother, Joyce Mwato, transform our family's financial future through her local Chama.

Every Saturday morning, I watched my grandmother walk miles to sit under a large acacia tree with twelve other women. Each carrying a small envelope containing 100 Kenyan Shillings - a significant amount for rural families during those difficult economic times.

That experience made me think about technology every day. I couldn't understand why such powerful community work required so much physical effort.

Under the Acacia Tree

"Bidii Women's Group" - where it all began. Twelve women, one dream, and the power of community that would inspire a digital revolution.

The Challenge

In 2010, when I was 11, my mother Judith Lily joined my grandmother's chama. Watching both of them walk those long distances to attend meetings broke my heart.

They never gave up, despite the challenges. Rain or shine, they would make that journey. Their dedication was inspiring, but I knew there had to be a better way.

Their commitment inspired me to dream of making life simpler for every mother out there who believed in the power of community savings.

Miles of Dedication

Every Saturday, watching my grandmother and mother walk miles for their chama meetings, I knew technology could solve this.

The Education

At Mount Kenya University, studying computer science, I carried this vision with me. Every algorithm I learned, every database concept I mastered, I thought about chamas.

I really felt bad remembering my grandmother and mother walking miles just to attend meetings. During university, I started building small tech solutions, always with community finance in mind.

I knew there had to be a way to preserve the beautiful spirit of chamas while eliminating the geographical barriers.

Mount Kenya University

Computer Science studies fueled by a childhood dream to digitize community finance and honor my grandmother's legacy.

The Vision Realized

Now at 25 years old, I've created this beautiful app that will help every chama across Africa. ChamaLink is my answer to those miles my grandmother and mother walked.

We've digitized that same spirit of collaboration I witnessed as a child, bringing modern tools including online meetings to traditional practices. No more walking miles - meet online!

I'm making life simple for every mother out there, just like my grandmother Joyce Mwato and my mother Judith Lily, who showed me the power of never giving up.

ChamaLink Today

1000+ chamas empowered, 15,000+ lives transformed, and the journey has just begun.

"Every feature we build, every decision we make, is guided by one principle: empowering your financial success. We're not just building software; we're honoring the legacy of every grandmother who walked miles for her chama."

Bilford Bwire, Founder & CEO of ChamaLink
